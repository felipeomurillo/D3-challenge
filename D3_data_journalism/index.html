Correlations discovered between health risks, age & income

In 2014, the United States Census Bureau conducted the American Community Survey (ACS) to gather information about how different health risks affect all 50 states in terms of income levels and median age.

The survey confirms what may already be common knowledge: states with higher levels of poverty also have higher populations lacking healthcare. Also, states with higher percentages of poverty have higher chances of obesity and smoking.

With respect to age, survey data can't be used to draw any significant correlations. The data does highlight that Texans significantly lack healthcare for being one of the younger states. Another point worth noting is Utah has the youngest population and the least number of smokers (per capita).

Finally, the median household income is a good predictor of obesity and smoking habits. States with more money in the household showed smaller numbers in obesity and smoking.
